## Building My Hugo Blog with the Help of AI

{{< newthought "As someone who appreciates the power of technology" >}}, I recently embarked on a journey to build my own blog. In this post, I'll share my experience collaborating with an AI language model to create my website using Hugo and tufte-css for styling.

### Choosing the Technology Stack

After careful consideration, I chose **Hugo** for static site generation due to its speed, flexibility, and ease of use. For styling, I opted for **tufte-css** to achieve a clean, elegant look inspired by Edward Tufte's design principles.

### Building the Base Template (baseof.html)

The foundation of my site is the base template, which features a global header and footer. Working with the AI language model, I crafted a responsive header design that adapts to different screen sizes. The site title aligns to the left, and the 'About' link to the right. Together, we also ensured that the footer remained anchored to the bottom of the screen.

### Building the Home Page (index.html)

For the home page, I wanted to display a list of post titles in reverse chronological order. With the AI model's guidance, I designed a template that generates a bulleted list, complete with publication dates and pagination.

### Lessons Learned & Challenges

Collaborating with an AI language model was a fascinating experience. We encountered challenges, such as adjusting the content width and anchoring the footer correctly. However, the model provided invaluable assistance in overcoming these hurdles and refining the site design.
